# Hormuz Importer Replacement Origins

Last updated: 2026-07-07.

## Bottom Line

The public record supports a replacement-origin story, but only at uneven resolution. Japan has the cleanest official crude-origin bridge, India has the cleanest official LPG supplier list and gas/LPG rationing evidence, China has the largest and most opaque adjustment mix, and South Korea remains mostly a supplier-assurance and inventory-buffer story rather than a public cargo-replacement story.

Use `data/derived/hormuz_f6r_7_replacement_supply_origins.csv` as the country-by-country matrix. It reconciles named origins with the existing F6R.5 bridge estimates, but it does not replace the caveat that the F6R.5 volumes are scenario allocations rather than observed vessel accounting.

## Blog-Ready Sentences

China: China probably used every flexible lever at once - Russian barrels, sanctioned or relabeled Iranian barrels, some Saudi/UAE bypass supply, commercial stocks, and lower refinery runs - but public data cannot allocate the bridge cleanly.

India: India publicly shifted crude toward non-Hormuz routes, but the clearer named-origin story is LPG: cargoes from the United States, Norway, Canada, Algeria, and Russia, plus extra domestic LPG yields, while gas/LPG rationing protected households first.

Japan: Japan is the cleanest reserve-plus-substitution case: METI named Yanbu, Fujairah, U.S. procurement, and possible Central Asia/South America supply, then estimated alternative procurement at roughly 60% of May and 70%+ of June would-have-been Hormuz crude.

South Korea: South Korea's public evidence is thinner: the visible response is Qatar LNG/condensate priority assurance, supplier diplomacy, and inventory buffers, not a verified crude replacement-origin table.

## Source Notes

- EIA/Vortexa route data provide 2024 destination aggregates for crude/condensate and LNG, not pairwise origin-to-destination cargo paths: [oil](https://www.eia.gov/todayinenergy/detail.php?id=65504), [LNG](https://www.eia.gov/todayinenergy/detail.php?id=65584).
- China evidence comes from CGEP's China energy security Q&A, EIA's China brief and stockpile analysis, and NBS refinery-run data. The important caveat is that Iranian relabeling, bonded storage, commercial stocks, and government SPR cannot be separated with public data alone.
- India evidence comes from PIB's March 11 briefing and March 12 parliamentary statement. These support the non-Hormuz crude route shift, gas/LPG rationing, and named LPG suppliers, but they do not name crude replacement countries.
- Japan evidence comes from METI press statements on March 3, March 13, March 24, and May 15. These are the strongest official replacement-route and reserve-release sources in the importer set.
- South Korea evidence comes mainly from MOTIR's June 16 Qatar release plus local F6R/S49 reserve notes. Treat Korea replacement volumes as modeled until customs, stock, or licensed cargo-flow data are reconciled.
